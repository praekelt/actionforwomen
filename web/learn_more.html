MAMA provides pregnant women and new mothers with
empowering, potentially life-saving information & support
through their mobile phones, across 5 mobile channels.

Mama on Mobi

This interactive, engaging mobi site will be available to all moms in South Africa with a Internet-enabled cellphone. Content will be delivered to users based on their due date or the age of their baby, providing personalised, relevant and compelling information to mothers.

Register at askmama.mobi

Mama on Sms

MAMA on SMS is a personalized SMS reminder service, aimed at supporting moms throughout their pregnancy, birth and during their childâ€™s first year. Due to the high cost of SMSs in South Africa, the MAMA program can unfortunately not offer this SMS service to moms at a national scale. The MAMA SMS service is currently available at selected clinics in Hillbrow, Johannesburg. The MAMA SA partners are working hard on bringing more funding and partnerships onboard, that will enable us to scale up the MAMA SMS service, to more clinics and hospitals throughout South Africa.

Mama on Ussd

MAMA on USSD is a set of interactive quizzes, aimed at supporting moms throughout their pregnancy, birth and during their child's first year. Any mom with a South African cellphone can register for the USSD quizzes, by dialing *120*2112#, and then she can dial back into this same line, every "MAMA Monday" to get a personalized quiz that will help her to learn about her pregnancy and baby. This quiz service is free for all moms on Telkom Mobile and Cell C. Vodacom and MTN users will pay 20 cents for 20 seconds to access the quizzes.

Mama on Mxit

Taking advantage of the more than 10 Million users on MXit in South Africa, MAMA will establish an educational portal on the social networking platform providing young men and women aged between 18 and 25 with important information just as pregnancy, childbirth and parenting becomes something many of them have to grapple with.

Mama on Voice

MAMA SA is testing the use of pre-recorded voice messages delivered to mothers in selected areas. A registered mom would receive a normal SMS notification that they have a new voicemail, and they would dial into their voicemail service and receiver their MAMA voice message, with relevant tips and information. MAMA on Voice is aimed at supporting moms who are illiterate, or have low levels of literacy.
